### Key Historical Facts from Sources

- **Premier's Announcement:** February 1920 - Premier Edward Granville Theodore announced support for the Dawson River irrigation scheme
- **Official Opening:** June 29, 1924 - Governor Sir Matthew Nathan turned on the irrigation water with Premier Theodore
- **Town Renaming:** December 1, 1926 - Castle Creek officially renamed Theodore in honor of the former Premier
- **Initial Settlement:** 264 irrigated farms (10-24 acres each) and 109 dry blocks opened in late 1926
- **Grand Vision:** Five proposed zones (Isla, Castle Creek, Huon, Moura, Coolibah) intended to support 5,000 farms and 50,000 people
- **Nathan Dam:** Planned as the centerpiece at Nathan Gorge to store 2,485,000 acre feet of water, submerging 83,200 acres - never built
- **Royal Commission:** March 1933 - Established to investigate the scheme which cost £1,053,057 (£8,492 per settler) for 124 remaining settlers
- **Commission Finding:** Declared the scheme "50 years ahead of its time" and recommended postponing Nathan Dam for 25 years
- **Irrigation Act:** The 1922 Irrigation Act provided the legislative framework for the scheme's implementation
