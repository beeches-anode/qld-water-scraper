## Summary

Sunwater has announced an auction for 5,610 megalitres of medium priority water allocations in the Mareeba-Dimbulah Water Supply Scheme, with parcels ranging from 5 to 200 megalitres. The auction, hosted on the Water Exchange platform with closing dates up to November 27, represents the final tranche of available allocations in the scheme. This follows the successful sale of 5,898 megalitres earlier in April and May 2024.

The water allocations were made available through water savings achieved by the $32.54 million Mareeba-Dimbulah Efficiency Improvement Project. This infrastructure modernization project, jointly funded by the Queensland Government ($20.94 million) and the Australian Government ($11.6 million), involved replacing 14km of pipeline, installing 125 automated control gates, and upgrading 157 water offtakes to enhance delivery efficiency across 17,000 hectares of farmland.
