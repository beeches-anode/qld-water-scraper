## Detailed Information Section

### Project Overview

The Haughton Pipeline Project is one of the most significant water infrastructure projects in Townsville's history, designed to provide long-term water security for North Queensland's largest city. The project consists of two major stages connecting the Burdekin River to Ross River Dam via the Haughton River, enabling water transfer during periods of low rainfall.

The project emerged from Townsville's water security challenges and was incorporated into the Townsville City Deal, Australia's first City Deal signed in 2016. This 15-year agreement between the Australian Government, Queensland Government, and Townsville City Council aimed to transform the city's economy through strategic infrastructure investment.

The pipeline's primary function is to "top up" Ross River Dam (capacity 250,000 megalitres) by pumping water from the reliable Burdekin River system during drought conditions. This addresses Townsville's vulnerability to rainfall variability and ensures water security for the city's growing population and industrial development.

**Key Stakeholders:**
- **Townsville City Council:** Project lead and co-funder
- **Queensland Government:** Major funding partner ($195 million)
- **Australian Government:** Originally committed funding through City Deal
- **BMD Group:** Principal contractor for Stage 2 construction
- **Iplex:** Major pipeline supplier
- **Bindal Aboriginal Corporation:** Traditional Owners with Cultural Heritage Management Agreement

### Key Specifications

- **Capacity/Scale:**
  - Pipeline capacity: 273 megalitres per day (minimum)
  - Future expansion capacity: 364 megalitres per day
  - Total pipeline length: Approximately 64 kilometers
  - Pipeline diameter: 1.8 meters

- **Infrastructure Components:**
  - **Stage 1 (Completed):** 36 kilometers of 1.8m diameter pipeline from Haughton River to Ross River Dam (2,763 individual pipe sections)
  - **Stage 2 (Under Construction):** 28 kilometers of 1.8m diameter pipeline from Haughton River to Burdekin River near Clare
  - Haughton Pump Station
  - Burdekin River crossing infrastructure
  - Haughton River crossing infrastructure
  - Approximately 6km of construction access roads (Stage 2)

- **Design Features:**
  - GRP (Glass Reinforced Plastic) Flowtite pipes
  - Designed for climate resilience and long-term durability
  - Modular construction allowing staged implementation
  - Future expansion capability built into design

- **Operational Parameters:**
  - Water sourced from Burdekin River during low rainfall periods
  - Pumped through Haughton system to Ross River Dam
  - Operates as supplementary supply during drought conditions
  - Supports both urban water supply and agricultural development

### Project History and Status

#### Early Development and Townsville City Deal (Pre-2016 - 2016)

Water security has been a long-standing concern for Townsville, which relies primarily on Ross River Dam. The concept of connecting to the more reliable Burdekin River system emerged from regional water security assessments.

In 2016, the Townsville City Deal was signed as Australia's first City Deal initiative. This landmark agreement brought together the Australian Government, Queensland Government, and Townsville City Council in a 15-year partnership to transform the city's economy. The Haughton Pipeline was identified as a critical infrastructure component.

#### Stage 1 Design and Construction (2017-2020)

Stage 1 development commenced in 2017 with detailed design work for the 36-kilometer pipeline between the Haughton River and Ross River Dam. The Queensland Government committed $215 million to this first stage.

Construction involved:
- Installation of 2,763 individual 1.8-meter diameter pipe sections
- Construction of the Haughton Pump Station
- Haughton River crossing infrastructure
- Extensive site preparation and access road construction

In June 2020, Stage 1 was completed successfully, delivering 36.5 kilometers of operational pipeline. This provided the foundational infrastructure for the eventual connection to the Burdekin River.

#### Stage 2 Planning and Approvals (2020-2023)

Following Stage 1 completion, attention turned to Stage 2, which would extend the pipeline a further 28 kilometers to the Burdekin River near Clare.

**Key milestones in Stage 2 development:**

- **November 2020:** Design tender for Stage 2 awarded, advancing detailed planning
- **2021:** Funding arrangements finalized - $274 million total budget ($195 million Queensland Government, $79 million Townsville City Council)
- **December 2021:** Bindal Traditional Owners conducted comprehensive cultural heritage survey of the 28-kilometer route
- **December 2022:** Cultural Heritage Management Agreement recommitted
- **February 2023:** Commonwealth EPBC (Environment Protection and Biodiversity Conservation) approval obtained
- **May 2023:** All bore drilling, ground surveys, and environmental survey work completed

The detailed business case for Stage 2 was developed and submitted to Infrastructure Australia for evaluation. The project was deemed critical for Townsville's water security and regional development.

#### Budget Challenges (2023-2024)

In early 2023, significant budget challenges emerged. The project was forecasting to exceed the approved budget of $274 million, with costs escalating to approximately $420 million - a $146 million overrun (53% increase).

The primary cause was identified as construction cost escalation following the COVID-19 pandemic. Material costs, labor costs, and supply chain disruptions contributed to the significant increase.

**Council's response:**
- Public acknowledgment of budget challenges (July 2024)
- Council stated it cannot contribute additional funds beyond the $79 million commitment
- Continued discussions with Queensland Government on funding solutions
- Commitment to project completion while seeking cost management strategies

Despite budget pressures, all stakeholders remained committed to completing the project given its critical importance to Townsville's water security.

#### Stage 2 Construction (2023-Present)

Construction of Stage 2 commenced in October 2023 with the first new pipes being laid. The work involves:
- Installation through predominantly greenfield terrain
- Construction of approximately 6km of access roads
- Burdekin River crossing infrastructure
- Connection to existing Stage 1 infrastructure

**Progress milestones:**
- **October 2023:** First pipes laid for Stage 2
- **May 2024:** More than 3km of pipe installed in a single month
- **August 2024:** 30% of Stage 2 pipeline completed
- **2024-2025:** Continued installation through greenfield terrain

Original completion target was end of 2024, later revised to 2025 for commissioning.

#### Current Status (November 2024)

The project is actively progressing with Stage 2 construction ongoing. As of the most recent reports (August 2024), 30% of Stage 2 has been completed. Installation continues through the greenfield terrain with work expected to continue throughout the 2024-25 financial year.

Stage 1 remains fully operational, providing the existing Haughton River to Ross River Dam connection capability while Stage 2 construction proceeds.

### First Nations and Cultural Heritage

**Traditional Owner Groups:**

The Haughton Pipeline route crosses the traditional lands of the **Bindal people**, represented by the **Bindal Aboriginal Corporation**.

**Cultural Heritage Assessment:**

A comprehensive cultural heritage assessment process was undertaken for Stage 2:

*December 2021:* Bindal Traditional Owners undertook a cultural heritage survey, walking the entire 28-kilometer proposed pipeline route. This survey represented a key milestone in ensuring cultural respect and protection.

**Elder Eddie Smallwood's Statement:**
"The cultural heritage survey and monitoring undertaken along the route of the pipeline ensured cultural respect was maintained and gave traditional owners an opportunity to get back on country."

This statement reflects the dual purpose of cultural heritage work: protecting significant sites while enabling Traditional Owners to maintain connection to country.

**Consultation Process:**

The consultation framework with Bindal Traditional Owners included:
- Early engagement in Stage 2 planning
- Negotiation of Cultural Heritage Management Agreement
- Direct participation in route survey and assessment
- Ongoing monitoring involvement during construction
- Regular communication and project updates

**Cultural Heritage Management Agreement:**

A **Cultural Heritage Management Agreement (CHMA)** was negotiated and signed with Bindal Traditional Owners. This agreement:
- Establishes framework for cultural heritage protection throughout project
- Defines roles and responsibilities for all parties
- Provides for Traditional Owner participation in monitoring
- Sets protocols for unexpected cultural heritage discoveries
- Includes dispute resolution mechanisms

**December 2022:** The CHMA was reviewed and recommitted, demonstrating ongoing commitment to the partnership.

**Cultural Heritage Concerns:**

While specific cultural sites are not detailed in public documents (appropriately protecting sensitive information), the comprehensive survey process addressed:
- Identification and protection of cultural sites along the route
- Assessment of potential impacts on cultural values
- Development of site-specific protection measures
- Protocols for construction near sensitive areas

**Protection Measures:**

- Cultural Heritage Management Agreement framework
- Traditional Owner participation in route selection and monitoring
- Cultural awareness training for construction workers
- Site-specific protection measures for identified cultural locations
- Protocols for managing unexpected discoveries during construction
- Ongoing Traditional Owner involvement throughout project lifecycle

**Ongoing Relationship:**

The project demonstrates positive engagement between Townsville City Council and Bindal Traditional Owners:
- Regular communication and consultation
- Traditional Owner employment and participation opportunities
- Respect for cultural protocols and knowledge
- Long-term relationship extending beyond project completion

**Significance:**

The Haughton Pipeline Project stands as one of North Queensland's most significant infrastructure investments, providing essential water security for Townsville's future. Despite budget challenges, the project demonstrates the value of proactive water infrastructure planning, successful multi-government partnerships, and respectful engagement with Traditional Owners.

As Stage 2 construction progresses toward 2025 completion, the project will deliver a critical long-term asset enabling Townsville's sustainable growth for generations to come. The lessons learned - both successes and challenges - will inform future water infrastructure projects across regional Australia.
